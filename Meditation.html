Meditation tips

Start Small

Begin with 2–5 minutes per session. It’s better to be consistent with short sessions than to burn out trying to meditate too long.

  • Calm

    Meditation and sleep app with specific programs for student stress and anxiety.

Pick a Regular Time

Resources to support exercise, nutrition, and overall physical health.

  • FitOn

    Free workout app with exercises that can be done in small spaces like dorm rooms.

Create a Quiet Space

Tools to enhance studying, time management, and learning effectiveness.

  • Forest

    Focus timer app that helps you stay off your phone during study sessions.

Focus on the Breath

Pay attention to the sensation of your breath coming in and going out. If your mind wanders (which it will), gently bring it back to the breath.

Use a Timer

Set a timer so you’re not constantly checking the clock. You can use apps like Insight Timer, Calm, or Headspace.

  • Mint

    Free budgeting app to track expenses and manage student finances.

Try Guided Meditation

If sitting in silence is difficult at first, guided meditations can help you stay focused. There are many free ones online..

  • Meetup

    Platform to find local groups based on interests and hobbies.
