Real stories from students who have transformed their college experience through self-care

The Impact of Self-Care

Reading about others' experiences can be both inspiring and informative. These testimonials offer a glimpse into how fellow students have incorporated self-care practices into their academic lives and the positive changes they've experienced as a result.

While everyone's journey is unique, these stories highlight common challenges students face and practical strategies that have helped them maintain wellbeing while pursuing their academic goals.

Sleep Improvement

Implementing a consistent sleep schedule completely transformed my academic experience. As a pre-med student, I used to wear sleep deprivation like a badge of honor, but I was actually sabotaging myself. Now I protect my 7 hours of sleep as non-negotiable, and my test scores have actually improved while I feel more present and engaged in my classes.

M

Morgan

Junior, Biology Major

After failing two midterms during my first semester, I realized my high school study habits weren't working. The time management and study strategies I learned through this site helped me restructure my approach completely. Instead of pulling all-nighters, I now study in focused blocks with actual breaks for meals and exercise. My GPA went from a 2.3 to a 3.7 in one semester!

J

James

Sophomore, Computer Science

I was experiencing intense anxiety during my first year, but I thought that was just part of the college experience. Learning mindfulness techniques and getting support through my university's counseling center changed everything. I still experience stress, but now I have tools to manage it before it becomes overwhelming. Don't wait until you're at a breaking point to prioritize your mental health.

L

Leila

Senior, Psychology

As a student athlete, I was constantly exhausted trying to balance training, classes, and social life. Learning proper nutrition and meal prep strategies helped me fuel my body appropriately and maintain energy throughout my demanding schedule. Simple changes like prepping protein-rich snacks and staying properly hydrated made a huge difference in my recovery and mental focus.

D

Darnell

Junior, Sports Management

As an international student, I felt incredibly isolated and homesick my first semester. Following the social wellness strategies helped me step outside my comfort zone and build connections on campus. Joining study groups and a cultural student organization gave me a sense of belonging that made all the difference in my college experience.

S

Sofia

Sophomore, International Relations

I used to think exercise was just something I didn't have time for as a double major. Finding small ways to incorporate movement into my day—walking between classes, doing quick dorm workouts, and attending one yoga class weekly—improved my focus, sleep quality, and overall mood. It actually creates more time than it takes by making me more efficient.

T

Tyler

Senior, Economics & Mathematics
