Student Nutrition Guide

This guide offers practical advice for students on maintaining a balanced diet, focusing on affordable, convenient, and nutritious options to improve academic performance and overall health.

Nutrition Basics for Students

  • Complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (whole grains, legumes, fruits, vegetables)
  • Lean proteins for focus and muscle maintenance (beans, eggs, poultry, fish, tofu)
  • Healthy fats for brain function (avocados, nuts, olive oil, fatty fish)
  • Fruits and vegetables for essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants
  • Adequate hydration for cognitive function and energy levels

Student-Friendly Meal Ideas

Budget-Friendly Shopping

Buy staples in bulk, choose frozen fruits and vegetables, shop store brands, and use student discounts at local grocery stores.

Meal Prep Strategies

Dedicate 1-2 hours on weekends to prepare components that can be mixed and matched throughout the week.

Dorm Room Cooking

Invest in a few key tools: microwave-safe containers, electric kettle, and mini fridge if possible.

Dining Hall Navigation

Build a balanced plate with lean protein, whole grains, and plenty of vegetables from the salad bar.

Healthy Convenience

Keep nutritious ready-to-eat items on hand for busy days: canned beans, pre-cooked rice packets, canned tuna.

Hydration Habits

Carry a reusable water bottle and aim to refill it several times throughout the day. Add fruit for flavor.
