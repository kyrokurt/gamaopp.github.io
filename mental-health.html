Mental Health for Students

This guide provides practical strategies and resources to help students maintain good mental health during college and university life.

Understanding Student Mental Health

Mental health is a spectrum, with 30-50% of college students experiencing significant distress. Awareness, early intervention, and self-care can improve outcomes, but persistent symptoms may require professional support.

Mental Health Strategies for Students

Manage Academic Stress

  • Break large projects into smaller tasks

  • Use a planner to track deadlines

  • Schedule regular study breaks

Prioritize Social Connection

  • Schedule regular time with friends

  • Maintain connections with family

  • Balance social time with alone time

Develop Healthy Coping Skills

  • Practice mindfulness and meditation

  • Try progressive muscle relaxation

  • Use deep breathing techniques during stress

Create Boundaries

  • Learn to say no when necessary

  • Set realistic goals and expectations

  • Communicate your needs clearly

Physical Health Connection

  • Prioritize regular sleep

  • Maintain balanced nutrition

  • Spend time in nature when possible

Seek Support When Needed

  • Familiarize yourself with campus resources

  • Talk to trusted friends or family

  • Remember seeking help is a sign of strength
